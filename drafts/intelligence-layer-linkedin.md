# LinkedIn Post — Intelligence Layer Wars

---

90% of marketing organizations now use AI agents in their stack.

And almost all of them are running on the same underlying models—OpenAI or Anthropic underneath, different logo on top.

The interfaces vary. The workflow branding varies. The reasoning engine is frequently identical to what the competitor down the street is renting from the same provider.

So what's left to compete on? Speed? That became table stakes in Q1.

This is the question I've been wrestling with while developing the AMP framework for my upcoming book with Wiley. The answer is becoming clearer: **the orchestration layer is commoditizing, but the intelligence layer is diverging.**

The enterprises building proprietary context—first-party data they actually own, business semantics, decision logic, the memory of what actually worked—are creating durable advantage.

The ones wrapping commodity AI in branded interfaces are not.

I wrote up the market evidence: Gartner's agent predictions, Forrester's "chase-catch gap," Scott Brinker's data on CDP dissolution, and what we're seeing inside SAP's enterprise customers.

The short version: the frameworks in *Agent Driven* aren't predictions anymore. They're descriptions of what's happening now.

Full essay: https://thefullstack.chrisohara.com/articles/intelligence-layer-wars.html

What layer of the stack is your organization investing in?
